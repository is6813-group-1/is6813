Capstone Final Project - Predictive Maintenece: Swire Coca-Cola

Introduction

This exploratory data analysis (EDA) focuses on understanding machine breakdowns and maintenance patterns in Swire Coca-Colas production plants. The data comes from the Internal Warehouse Controller (IWC) system, which tracks machine downtimes, repairs, and maintenance activities across multiple locations.

Problem Statement:

Swire Coca-Cola faces significant losses, around $60 million annually, due to unplanned machine breakdowns. The company wants to reduce these losses by identifying patterns in downtime and developing a predictive maintenance model that can foresee breakdowns before they happen. This would allow the company to better plan for repairs and minimize downtime.

Objectives:

The key objectives of this analysis are to: - Analyze the distribution of machine downtime across different plants and machines. - Compare the effectiveness of preventive vs.unplanned maintenance. - Explore how downtime impacts productivity. - Identify any seasonal or time-based patterns in breakdowns. - Gather insights that will help build a predictive maintenance model to reduce future downtime.

This EDA will lay the groundwork for more advanced analysis aimed at optimizing maintenance schedules and improving production efficiency.

Questions

  1. Downtime Distribution and Patterns
    • What is the distribution of downtime (in minutes) across different plants and machines?
  2. Predictive Maintenance Insights
    • Which machines or parts are most prone to breakdowns? (Using EQUIPMENT_ID and MAINTENANCE_ITEM)
  3. Maintenance Type and Effectiveness
    • What percentage of maintenance tasks are preventive (planned) versus corrective (unplanned)? Does preventive maintenance reduce unplanned downtime?
  4. Operational Impact
    • What is the overall impact of downtime on productivity (based on ACTUAL_WORK_IN_MINUTES)? Can we quantify the opportunity cost per machine or plant?
  5. Temporal Analysis
    • Are there any seasonal trends in downtime frequency (e.g., more breakdowns during specific months or seasons)?

# Plot downtime distribution

ggplot(non_zero_downtime, aes(x = ACTUAL_WORK_IN_MINUTES)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 5, fill = "gray", color = "red") +
  coord_cartesian(xlim = c(0, 100)) +
  labs(title = "Distribution of Downtime", 
       x = "Downtime (Minutes)", 
       y = "Count")

Insights The distribution of downtime reveals significant peaks around 20, 50, and 90 minutes, indicating that many breakdowns are resolved quickly, while others take longer due to potentially more complex issues. Short downtimes around 20 minutes may reflect minor malfunctions or efficient repairs, while the peaks at 50 and 90 minutes suggest recurring issues that take longer to resolve. Understanding these patterns can help Swire Coca-Cola optimize its maintenance operations by addressing the most frequent and time-consuming problems. By targeting these downtime intervals, the company can reduce operational disruptions and improve overall efficiency. This insight supports a more data-driven approach to predictive maintenance.

Time to Resolve Orders by Plants

# Calculate mean and median of downtime per location
avg_downtime_by_location <- data %>%
  group_by(PLANT_ID) %>%
  summarize(
    mean_downtime = mean(ACTUAL_WORK_IN_MINUTES, na.rm = TRUE),
    median_downtime = median(ACTUAL_WORK_IN_MINUTES, na.rm = TRUE),
    count = n()
  ) %>%
  arrange(desc(mean_downtime))

Results: - This exploratory data analysis provided valuable insights into machine downtime and maintenance patterns within Swire Coca-Colas production plants. The following key findings emerged from the analysis:

  • Downtime Distribution: Downtime across machines tends to cluster around 20, 50, and 90 minutes, suggesting that certain breakdowns are resolved quickly, while others take significantly longer, potentially due to more complex issues. By focusing on these frequent downtime intervals, Swire Coca-Cola can identify and address recurring issues to improve operational efficiency.

  • Work Orders by Plant: Plant G261 stands out with the highest number of work orders, indicating either a larger operational scale or more frequent breakdowns compared to other plants. Addressing the inefficiencies or higher maintenance needs at this plant could yield significant improvements. Plants G221 and G291 also have notable volumes of work orders, which warrants further investigation into their maintenance practices.

  • Time to Resolve Orders: The analysis revealed that plants with fewer work orders but longer downtimes may face operational challenges or inefficiencies in their maintenance processes. For example, plants with similar workloads but shorter downtimes may have more effective repair processes. Optimizing maintenance practices across plants with high downtime could help streamline operations and reduce costs.

  • Equipment Maintenance Patterns: A significant data quality issue was observed, with many work orders associated with missing or improperly recorded equipment IDs. Addressing these data quality issues would enhance Swire Coca-Colas ability to track and optimize maintenance activities for specific machines.

  • Maintenance Type Effectiveness: Preventative and corrective maintenance orders accounted for the majority of work orders. Preventative maintenance is expected to reduce unplanned downtime, but further analysis is needed to confirm the effectiveness of these activities. Prioritizing preventative efforts can improve equipment longevity and reduce corrective maintenance needs.

  • Downtime by Maintenance Type: The analysis of downtime by maintenance type revealed notable differences in how long specific types of maintenance activities take. Some maintenance types exhibited significantly longer downtimes, indicating either more complex repairs or less efficient processes. These findings suggest an opportunity for process improvement to reduce downtime duration.

  • Temporal Analysis: The temporal analysis did not uncover any clear seasonal patterns in downtime, but further analysis could be beneficial to explore potential factors influencing machine breakdowns over time.

  • Correlation Analysis: The correlation analysis provided a preliminary understanding of relationships between key numeric variables in the dataset. While no extreme correlations were found, certain variables showed moderate correlations that could inform further modeling efforts.
